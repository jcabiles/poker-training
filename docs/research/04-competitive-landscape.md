# Competitive Landscape: Poker Training Products & Community Sentiment

**Research date:** 2026-06-27
**Purpose:** Supplement UX/learning-science research with a competitive and community lens. Feed into local-app design decisions. Research only — no design or code.

---

## PART A — Product Survey

### 1. GTO Wizard

**Core features:**
- Full preflop and postflop range explorer covering all streets
- GTO Trainer with instant feedback and one-click hand history analysis
- 10+ million pre-solved GTO solutions for cash games, MTT, Spin & Go, heads-up SNG
- Mass hand history upload to detect personal weaknesses
- PokerArena (launched mid-2025): free competitive heads-up platform with automatic hand analysis
- "Single Size Solutions" — AI-driven simplified strategies across all paid tiers
- Ultra tier: Multiway AI Solver and Custom Preflop Tree Builder

**Target audience:** All skill levels; marketed as suitable for beginners through high-stakes professionals. Primary use case is serious study by players who want solver-accurate outputs.

**Learning approach:** Explore solutions → drill specific spots → review hand histories against GTO benchmarks. Active practice model with instant feedback loops.

**Pricing (as of March 2026 restructure):**
- Free: limited access
- Starter: ~$39/month (includes 200+ hours recorded coaching, weekly sessions)
- Premium: ~$99/month
- Elite: ~$129/month
- Ultra: ~$206/month (Multiway AI Solver, custom tree builder)
- Annual billing: ~25% discount

**Strengths:**
- Largest pre-solved solution library in the industry; covers cash games comprehensively
- Instant, spot-specific feedback without external solver setup
- Unified platform (study + drill + hand review)
- Active development; product shipping new features continuously

**Weaknesses:**
- Steep learning curve for solver novices; players with no solver experience often feel overwhelmed
- Pricing sits at premium end — hardest to justify for low-stakes recreational grinders
- Heavy GTO orientation; limited coaching on how to deviate exploitatively against weak opponents
- Not designed for live-specific nuances (no limper modeling, live tell integration, etc.)

**Community verdict:** Widely considered the market leader for solver-based study. Recommended as the "one tool if you had to choose one" for serious students. Low-stakes players are told it's useful but that the cost is harder to justify without a real study commitment.

### 2. PokerCoaching.com (Jonathan Little)

**Core features:**
- 70+ courses; 1,800+ hand quizzes with immediate feedback
- Four exclusive masterclasses (cash game beginner, cash game advanced, MTT early/mid, MTT bubble/late)
- Monthly homework with community feedback
- Weekly live coaching sessions with elite professionals
- Range Analyzer, Push/Fold Charts, PKO Bounty Calculator tools
- Integrated PeakGTO solver training within platform
- Active Discord community; 120,000+ registered users, 5,700+ active community members

**Target audience:** Beginner to intermediate. Explicitly serves both cash game and tournament players, though community perception leans tournament-heavy. Strong for recreational to mid-level grinders.

**Learning approach:** Structured curriculum → quiz-based active practice → solver-backed drills within the platform. Milestone progression system. "Learn by deciding, then get feedback" model — more interactive than pure video.

**Pricing:**
- Free: 20 quizzes, 10 classes, limited sessions
- Standard: ~$39/month (1,800+ quizzes, 70+ courses)
- Premium: ~$118/month (full access + masterclasses)
- 30-day money-back guarantee

**Strengths:**
- Best-in-class interactive quiz volume (1,500+ quizzes; 6.5M+ taken by community)
- Structured roadmap with clear progression — not a loose video library
- Most integrated solver drill experience of any content platform (PeakGTO)
- Strong community accountability via Discord
- Affordable entry point; free tier is genuinely useful
- Cash game masterclass explicitly covers beginner small/mid-stakes cash

**Weaknesses:**
- Tournament bias in instructor roster and content emphasis
- Less video-centric; players who prefer long-form "watch and absorb" may find it light
- Live poker vs. online distinctions are not deeply covered
- Advanced players seeking nose-bleed content are underserved

**Community verdict:** Consistently ranked #1 or #2 in head-to-head reviews (scored 91/100 vs Upswing's 69/100 in one detailed comparison). Praised for interactivity. Seen as the best choice for structured learners who want to be tested, not just lectured.

### 3. Upswing Poker (Lab 2.0 + courses)

**Core features:**
- Upswing Lab 2.0: 30+ foundational strategy modules, advanced strategies, Play & Explains (coaches demonstrating live hands), MTT content, live poker section (20 videos across 3 sections)
- Mini-courses on specialized topics
- Weekly Q&A sessions, live strategy breakdowns
- Private Discord server
- Supplemental standalone courses ($299–$999 each) covering heads-up, PLO matrix, mixed games, etc.
- Starting hand charts included
- Free preflop range app (separate)

**Target audience:** Micro to low-stakes online players moving up; intermediate cash game grinders. Less suited to absolute beginners or established high-stakes players.

**Learning approach:** Primarily video-based "watch, absorb, apply" model. Requires self-directed study planning; limited built-in drills compared to PokerCoaching. Updated weekly with new Play & Explains and monthly strategy modules.

**Pricing:**
- Lab 2.0: $99/month; $399/six months; $699/year
- No free trial
- Additional courses: $299–$999 each (not included in Lab)
- 30-day money-back guarantee

**Strengths:**
- High-quality video instruction from known pros (Doug Polk, Ryan Fee, Fried Meulders)
- Strong brand reputation; large, engaged community
- Live poker section (rare among competitors)
- Comprehensive starting-hand framework
- Play & Explains are genuinely useful for seeing thought process in action

**Weaknesses:**
- Primarily video-only; limited built-in interactive drills (weaker than PokerCoaching on this axis)
- Requires self-directed study discipline — no structured roadmap or adaptive sequencing
- Expensive relative to interactive alternatives; additional premium courses stack cost significantly
- Platform described as "a bit outdated" in UI/UX
- No free trial creates adoption friction

**Community verdict:** Respected for content quality but criticized for being passive and expensive. Best suited to players who are self-disciplined enough to structure their own study. Cash game focus is genuine; Upswing wins on cash game content breadth vs PokerCoaching in most comparisons.

### 4. Red Chip Poker (CORE 2.0 / Pro / Crash Courses)

**Core features:**
- CORE 2.0: 200+ lessons, structured A-to-Z curriculum, 20+ quizzes with feedback, achievements and badges, multiple coaches, in-depth hand breakdowns
- Dedicated "Live $1/$2 Basics" and "Live $1/$2 NL Crash Course" crash courses ($39.95 one-time)
- "GTO Crash Course" for solver-curious players
- Red Chip Poker Discord: community hand posting, feedback, live events
- Pro subscription: expanded video library, additional coaches
- Concept-driven teaching style over rote memorization

**Target audience:** Explicitly small-stakes and live cash game players. CORE is marketed as a foundational course for anyone who knows the rules. Crash courses aimed directly at $1/$2 live players.

**Learning approach:** Concept-first teaching. Self-paced, relaxed tone. "Learn at your own pace; mistakes are learning, not failure." Structured sequencing from fundamentals upward.

**Pricing:**
- CORE 2.0: ~$5/week ($20/month equivalent); most affordable comprehensive curriculum in the market
- Crash courses: $39.95 one-time
- Pro subscription: $50/month (7-day free trial)

**Strengths:**
- Only major platform with an explicit $1/$2 live cash focus (crash course)
- Most affordable structured curriculum ($5/week is hard to beat)
- Concept clarity — coaches known for explaining "why," not just "what"
- GTO Crash Course bridges theory and solver work for non-experts
- Free Discord community is active and beginner-friendly
- Low commitment entry (crash courses are standalone one-time purchases)

**Weaknesses:**
- Less famous/prominent than Upswing or PokerCoaching (smaller student base, less social proof)
- Library smaller than top competitors
- Less solver-integrated than PokerCoaching or GTO Wizard
- Pro tier content less comprehensive than Upswing Lab

**Community verdict:** Consistently praised by low-stakes and live cash players as the most relevant and affordable option. "The friendliest poker community." Seen as underrated relative to its quality. The $1/$2 crash course is the most cited product for the exact player profile we're building for.

### 5. Advanced Poker Training (APT)

**Core features:**
- AI-driven simulation: play hundreds of hands per hour against realistic AI opponents
- "Brain" overlay: reveals decision factors (pot odds, stack ratios, opponent modeling) during play
- Combat Trainer: shove/fold module
- Beat the Pro challenges
- Weekly performance reports identifying individual leaks with targeted exercises
- 1-on-1 coaching sessions with professionals (Jonathan Little, Kenna James, etc.)
- Printable odds charts and starting hand guides
- GTO-style bots and exploitative bots available
- 9-max, 6-max, Sit-n-Gos, MTTs, Heads Up, WSOP Main Event formats
- Free entry-level resources available

**Target audience:** Players who learn by doing rather than watching. Beginners through intermediate; explicitly designed for live casino players (marketing language emphasizes casino-style play).

**Learning approach:** "Learning by doing" — high-volume simulation with contextual feedback during play. Closest to actual table repetitions of any platform. Addresses the passive-learning problem head-on.

**Pricing:**
- $39.97/month (discounts for longer commitments); free tier available
- Founded 2009; oldest AI-simulation poker trainer

**Strengths:**
- Only platform where you actually play and receive feedback simultaneously
- High hand volume per session (up to 500 hands/hour)
- Bridges theory-to-practice gap better than any video or quiz platform
- Patented technology; unique AI architecture
- Strong cash game simulation; relatively strong live game feel
- Affordable vs GTO Wizard or PokerCoaching premium tiers

**Weaknesses:**
- Website navigation criticized as clunky
- AI bots, while realistic, don't represent modern online regulars (better for live game emulation, ironically)
- Not a solver; cannot explain frequencies or range splits the way GTO Wizard can
- Less community engagement; no major Discord or quiz-feedback loop
- Content aging; founded 2009 and less aggressively updated than newer entrants

**Community verdict:** Generally positive for people who like learning by playing. Particularly praised by live players who find pure theory study hard to apply. Criticism centers on the interface and the gap between APT bots and real online regulars. Seen as complementary to, not a replacement for, a solver/quiz platform.

### 6. PokerSnowie

**Core features:**
- Neural network AI (trained by self-play, not true solver computation)
- Hand review: input hands and receive AI feedback and error rate scores
- Challenge the AI directly in simulated play
- Error Rate feedback loop: motivating metric for improvement
- Desktop and mobile (Android/iOS)

**Target audience:** Recreational players and beginners wanting simple, friendly feedback. Was once the premier AI coach; now positioned below solver-based tools.

**Learning approach:** AI feedback on individual decisions; error rate tracking. Simpler interface than solvers. "Approximate what GTO looks like" rather than compute it exactly.

**Pricing:** ~$29.90/month (or ~$200/year). Awkward price positioning vs modern tools.

**Strengths:**
- Genuinely beginner-friendly interface; simplest feedback loop in the market
- Error Rate metric is motivating and easy to understand
- Good for identifying fundamental leaks in loose-passive play (overvaluing weak pairs, excessive calling)
- Lower barrier to entry than solver platforms

**Weaknesses:**
- Not a true solver — neural network that approximates, doesn't compute equilibrium
- Last meaningful engine update: 2016; iOS app last updated July 2018 (2.4 stars App Store)
- Restricted to four fixed bet sizes; cannot model frequency splits or polarized sizing
- Known "limper blindspot": Snowie rarely open-limps so its model is weak against limper-heavy live games
- Cannot teach exploitative strategies; useless against weak players once you think in ranges
- Price-to-value vs modern competitors is poor: GTO Gecko (solver-accurate) is cheaper at $24.99/month
- Community verdict: "Respect the pioneer, study with a solver." Rated 6/10 in 2026 reviews

**Community verdict:** Near-universal consensus that PokerSnowie has been superseded. Acceptable as a first tool for absolute beginners who find solvers intimidating, but players outgrow it the moment they start thinking in ranges. Not recommended for our player profile.

### 7. DTO Poker Trainer (Dominik Nitsche)

**Core features:**
- GTO Trainer with pre-solved scenarios for cash games and tournaments
- Cash Game Trainer: simplified GTO strategies designed for practical table execution (not raw solver complexity)
- Tournament Product: GTO Trainer + Explorer for preflop ranges and multiway spots
- Emphasis on "highest-EV simplified strategies" — easier to execute at the table
- Mobile-first; available on App Store
- Free access available to start

**Target audience:** MTT players primarily; cash game module available. Players who want GTO strategies simplified for in-game execution rather than deep study.

**Learning approach:** Drill specific spots against pre-solved solutions. Focuses on simplified, executable strategies rather than full solver trees.

**Pricing:** ~$9.99/month (cheapest legitimate solver-based drilling tool in the market)

**Strengths:**
- Extremely affordable for solver-backed training
- Emphasis on simplification for real-game execution is exactly right for our player profile
- High solution quality (solutions "mirror strategies used by the best")
- Good for drilling preflop and common postflop spots quickly

**Weaknesses:**
- MTT-first in practice; cash game module is less developed than MTT
- Smaller library than GTO Wizard
- Less community and coaching integration
- Limited explorer functionality vs GTO Wizard

**Community verdict:** Praised for affordability and drilling efficiency. Often cited alongside Postflop+ as a budget-friendly solver trainer. Less discussed than GTO Wizard in cash game communities.

### 9. Equilab / Flopzilla / PokerStove (Equity & Analysis Tools)

**Equilab:**
- Free equity calculator (range vs range, hand vs range, hand vs hand)
- Range construction and visualization
- "Power Equilab" advanced version: ~$42/year
- Best for: Beginners learning to think in ranges and calculate equity
- Community verdict: "Free, accurate, essential first tool." Rated highly as beginner introduction.

**Flopzilla:**
- Range visualization tool: shows exactly how often a range hits each flop texture, broken into hand categories (top pair, two pair, flush draw, etc.)
- Heads-up and multiway equity calculations
- Price: $25 one-time (perpetual license; Pro version included)
- Best for: Intermediate players doing serious range-vs-board analysis
- Limitation: Desktop-only; no mobile version; no built-in drilling
- Community verdict: "Go-to study tool for serious players." Considered essential for understanding board coverage and range construction. Rated higher than Equilab for intermediate+ work.

**PokerStove:**
- Legacy free equity calculator; largely superseded by Equilab
- Still available; useful for quick hand-vs-hand equity math
- No longer actively developed

**Community notes:**
- Equilab vs Flopzilla debate: Equilab for beginners (simpler, free), Flopzilla for intermediate+ (more powerful range visualization)
- Neither teaches strategy — they are analysis tools requiring the user to know what questions to ask
- Smart Poker Study's "66 Days of Hand Reading" program built around Flopzilla Pro — widely cited community recommendation

## PART B — Community Sentiment

### What Players Praise (Features That Genuinely Helped)

**Interactivity and active practice over passive video:**
The single most consistent praise signal across forums, reviews, and comparison articles is that interactive, decision-forcing practice actually improves play while passive video watching does not. PokerCoaching's quiz engine (1,500+ quizzes; 6.5M taken) is the most-praised feature of any content platform. APT's simulation play is praised specifically by live players who find video study hard to translate.

**Instant feedback with EV context:**
Players consistently praise tools that immediately show not just "right or wrong" but *how much* a mistake cost (in EV). GTO Wizard's feedback loop, LeakSeek's EV differential display, and APT's "Brain" overlay all draw positive attention for making mistakes feel concrete and instructive.

**Structured progression and roadmaps:**
Community members frequently lament getting lost in large video libraries. PokerCoaching's milestone system and Red Chip CORE's A-to-Z sequencing are praised as rare examples of platforms that tell you what to study next. "I stopped feeling like I was just picking random videos" is a recurring sentiment.

**Affordable entry points:**
Red Chip CORE ($5/week) and the free tiers of PokerCoaching and GTO Wizard receive strong community praise specifically from low-stakes players who can't justify $100+/month subscriptions.

**Community/Discord for hand review:**
Active study communities (PokerCoaching Discord, Red Chip Discord, RecPoker study groups) are praised as multiplying the value of the platform itself. Players cite peer hand review as more impactful than content consumption alone.

**Mobile offline drilling:**
Postflop+ and Poker Trainer get explicit community praise for offline functionality. "I drill at the airport and in waiting rooms — that's when I actually study" is a representative comment.

---

### What Players Complain About

**GTO overkill for low stakes:**
This is the most common complaint from $1/$2–$2/$5 live players. Community consensus, supported by pro commentary, is that learning pure GTO before understanding exploitative play against specific opponent types is cart-before-horse at low stakes. "Opponents at $1/$2 don't play balanced ranges so studying balance is irrelevant" is a recurring forum position. Multiple training sites have been criticized for pushing GTO frameworks on players who would profit more from simple exploitative adjustments.

**Passive video learning that doesn't transfer:**
Research shows retention from passive viewing is low, and this finding is echoed constantly in poker forums. "I've watched 200 hours of training videos and I'm still not winning" is a cliché complaint in r/poker and 2+2. APT's origin story literally uses this frustration as its marketing premise. The gap between "understanding when detached from the game" and "executing at the table" is real and widely acknowledged.

**Cost stacking across tools:**
Players need multiple subscriptions to cover the full study stack (coaching site + solver + analysis tool + tracking software), and the cost adds up to $300–500/month for a complete setup. Community complaint: no single affordable platform covers everything a serious low-stakes player needs.

**No live-game-specific training:**
Nearly every tool is designed for or calibrated to online poker. Hand trackers (PokerTracker 4, HM3) are useless without hand histories from online play. HUDs don't exist in live play. The gap between software training environments and actual live game conditions is consistently identified as an unmet need. Even APT's bots don't model the specific patterns common in live $1/$2 games (limpers, fish calling stations, nit-tight regulars, recreational aggro players).

**Overwhelm and lack of personalization:**
Large video libraries (BBZ Poker's 2,353+ archived videos, Upswing's hundreds of modules) are criticized for creating "where do I start?" paralysis. Players without a structured roadmap churn through content without targeted improvement. The community consistently wants tools that diagnose their specific leaks and tell them exactly what to study.

**GTO tools don't teach exploitative adjustments:**
PokerSnowie's known "limper blindspot" is the canonical example, but this critique extends broadly. Solvers are designed to play unexploitably; they cannot teach you how to deviate from equilibrium against the specific villain in seat 3 who open-limps 80% of hands and only raises premiums. This gap is especially acute for live cash players.

### What Low-Stakes and Moving-Up Players Specifically Recommend

From Reddit, forum discussions, and coach recommendations aggregated across sources:

1. **Red Chip Poker CORE** — most commonly cited as the best structured foundation for $1/$2–$2/$3 live cash players. Affordable, concept-driven, explicitly live-game-relevant.
2. **Red Chip Poker "Live $1/$2 Crash Course"** — standalone one-time purchase widely cited as the most targeted product for this exact player type.
3. **PokerCoaching.com Standard tier** — praised for quiz interactivity and cash game masterclass content. Seen as the step up from CORE.
4. **GTO Wizard (Starter tier)** — recommended for players who have fundamentals down and want to study specific spots. Caveat: "don't start here; start with exploitative basics."
5. **Flopzilla** — "66 Days of Hand Reading" program built around it is widely cited as a transformative study project for moving from novice to thinking player.
6. **Equilab (free)** — standard recommendation as first equity tool, before moving to Flopzilla.
7. **Postflop+ (ThinkGTO)** — recommended for players who want mobile drilling; praised specifically for offline capability.
8. **APT** — recommended specifically by players who find video study frustrating and learn better by playing.
9. **SplitSuit's "Low-Stakes Poker Playbook"** — IF/THEN exploitative plays in book format, praised for live $1/$2–$2/$5 applicability.

---

### Recurring Unmet Needs and Gaps

The following gaps appear repeatedly across community sources and review sites:

1. **No tool teaches exploitative play against live player archetypes** — The community explicitly acknowledges that GTO tools teach balance; nobody teaches a structured framework for exploiting the specific player types you encounter in live $1/$2 games (LAG aggros, nit-tight regs, calling station fish, etc.).

2. **No live-player hand history capture tool with integrated analysis** — Pokerscope exists but is nascent. No tool combines: (a) easy hand entry at a live table, (b) post-session review, (c) equity calculation, (d) study integration. Online players have full tracking; live players have nothing equivalent.

3. **Retention tools for poker concepts are absent from major platforms** — Poker Toolkit exists as a standalone, but no major training platform integrates spaced repetition. Players learn concepts in videos and forget them. The SM-2 spaced repetition algorithm is proven for language learning but nearly absent from poker training.

4. **Adaptive/personalized drill sequencing based on actual leaks** — Most platforms offer static curricula. Only LeakSeek and PreflopAI are explicitly building adaptive systems that identify your weak spots and feed you relevant drills. GTO Wizard's mass upload feature does this partially, but requires uploading hand histories (not possible for live players).

5. **No integration of GTO baseline + exploitative deviation training** — The community wants a tool that teaches: "here is what GTO says, here is WHY you should deviate against this player type, here is how much EV you gain by exploiting them." No existing product delivers this coherently.

6. **Affordable all-in-one solution for live cash players** — The full recommended study stack (coaching site + solver + analysis tool) costs $200–400/month. Low-stakes players cannot justify this ROI. A single affordable tool covering fundamentals + drilling + hand review + session tracking would fill a major gap.

## PART C — Synthesis for Our Build

### "EMULATE THIS" List

**E1. Instant EV feedback on decisions (not just right/wrong)**
Every well-received training tool shows the player not just their mistake but how much it cost in expected value. Implement this in all drilling modules.

**E2. Active decision-forcing drills as the primary learning modality**
Do not make videos the core. Video as explainer; quiz/drill as the primary learning unit. The community's most consistent praise goes to interactive, decision-based platforms (PokerCoaching quizzes, APT simulation, Postflop+ drilling).

**E3. Structured progression — tell the player what to study next**
Upswing's main weakness is the absence of a structured roadmap. PokerCoaching's milestone system and Red Chip CORE's A-to-Z sequencing are praised specifically. Build adaptive progression that sequences content based on demonstrated weaknesses.

**E4. Spaced repetition for concept retention**
Poker Toolkit applies SM-2 to poker concepts; it exists only as a standalone niche tool. Integrating spaced repetition into a drilling system (resurface spots you missed; fade spots you've mastered) is a genuine gap no major platform has filled.

**E5. Exploitative play framing, not just GTO**
The most consistent community complaint is GTO overkill at low stakes. Frame all content as "here is the balanced baseline, here is when and why to deviate exploitatively against the player types you face in live $1/$2." The SplitSuit Playbook's IF/THEN format is the right model for live player content.

**E6. Player archetype training**
No existing tool systematically teaches adjustments against: calling station, nit-tight reg, LAG aggro, passive fish, positionally-unaware player. Live low-stakes games are full of predictable archetypes. Building drills organized by villain type would be uniquely valuable and has no direct competitor.

**E7. Session log + hand capture for live players**
Pokerscope is the only tool attempting this; it's nascent. A lightweight session logger (date, game, duration, key hands, result) plus quick hand entry with post-session review is a real gap the community has identified.

**E8. Concept explanations that show the "why" before the "what"**
Red Chip Poker's concept-driven teaching is specifically praised over "memorize this range" approaches. Explain the principle, then drill the application.

**E9. Affordable or free access**
The most-praised products in the low-stakes community are either free (Equilab), very cheap (CORE at $5/week), or have generous free tiers (PokerCoaching free tier). A local app with no paywall competes on this dimension by default.

**E10. Short, focused sessions compatible with real life**
LeakSeek's "5-minute focused sessions" design is praised by players who can't block 2-hour study sessions. Design for 10–20 minute daily practice, not marathon study.

---

### "AVOID THIS" List

**A1. Video-heavy passive curriculum as the primary experience**
The community is emphatic: passive video consumption does not transfer to the table. Videos should explain; drills should teach. Don't build a video library and call it training.

**A2. Pure GTO framing for a low-stakes audience**
GTO is cart-before-horse for $1/$2–$2/$3 players. Framing everything as "the GTO solution" creates overwhelm and irrelevance when facing opponents who don't remotely play balanced ranges.

**A3. Large content libraries without progression structure**
BBZ Poker's "2,353+ archived videos" is cited as a weakness — where to start? Don't build a content repository; build a learning path.

**A4. Pricing structures that require stacking subscriptions**
The $300–500/month total cost of a complete study stack is a community pain point. A local app is free by definition; don't design features that simulate subscription paywalls within the app.

**A5. Tools that assume online hand history data**
HUDs, hand trackers, and import-based analysis (including GTO Wizard's mass upload feature) require online hand histories. Our player is a live player. Don't design any feature that requires capturing hand histories that don't exist.

**A6. Complexity front-loading that overwhelms beginners**
GTO Wizard is praised by experts but specifically noted as overwhelming for solver novices. Don't require users to understand solver theory before accessing useful training. Start simple; layer complexity progressively.

**A7. Outdated or stale bots/AI (PokerSnowie anti-pattern)**
PokerSnowie's 2016 engine and 2018 iOS update are held up as cautionary examples. If we include AI opponents, they must reflect how actual live $1/$2 players behave — limper-heavy, calling-station tendencies, unbalanced ranges.

**A8. No feedback on exploitative deviations**
Most solvers only show GTO. Don't build a system that tells players they're "wrong" for making a clearly +EV exploitative deviation against a weak opponent.

---

### "GAPS WE COULD FILL" List

**G1. Live-player-first design** — The entire market is built for online players. A tool designed around the live $1/$2–$2/$3 experience (limpers, multiway pots, slower pace, no HUD, live reads) would be uniquely positioned. Every competitor leaves this audience underserved.

**G2. Exploitative archetype system** — Structured drills organized by villain type (calling station, nit, LAG, passive fish) that teach exploitative adjustments rather than just GTO baselines. No competitor does this systematically.

**G3. Integrated spaced repetition** — Combine concept learning + spaced flashcard review in one system. No major platform does this; players currently need Poker Toolkit as a separate tool.

**G4. Session log + quick hand entry** — Lightweight live session tracking (game context, key hand descriptions, result, notes) with post-session review workflow. Pokerscope attempts this but is nascent. This fills a genuine daily-use need for live players.

**G5. "GTO baseline + why to deviate" framing** — Every drill presents: (a) the GTO-informed default, (b) the opponent context that changes the answer, (c) the exploitative adjustment. No existing product delivers this as an integrated learning experience.

**G6. No paywall, no subscription fatigue** — The biggest structural advantage of a local personal app. Community sentiment shows clear resentment of $100+/month subscriptions. Being completely free removes the most common friction in committing to a training regimen.

**G7. Short-session mobile-compatible design** — Most web platforms assume desktop study sessions. A system designed for 10–15 minute focused sessions, mobile-friendly, would serve the live player lifestyle better than any current competitor.

**G8. Personalized leak tracking without hand history imports** — Build a system that identifies leaks through self-reported decisions and quiz performance rather than requiring hand history uploads. This is the only path to personalization for live players.
